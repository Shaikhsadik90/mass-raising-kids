About Mass Raising Kids

Mass Raising Kids was created to serve families struck by sudden loss. When a father dies due to illness, accident or other reasons, many mothers face overwhelming financial and emotional pressure.

Why we exist

We saw smart, capable children dropping out of school simply because the household could not handle tuition, travel or basic educational costs. Our goal is to bridge that gap — not just with short-term money, but with disciplined mentorship, academic tutoring and community accountability.

Our approach

Every referral goes through an intake that collects background documents, school reports, and a family interview. Our small evaluation team performs regular home visits and liaises with teachers to ensure the child's progress. If any red flags (addiction, neglect, or severe instability) are present, we pause support until the case meets our baseline standards.

Our Mission

To raise children with dignity, education and strong values — ensuring their future doesn’t break because of a tragedy. We believe in long-term outcomes: staying with a child across school years, providing mentorship, and building life skills that help them break the cycle of vulnerability.

Education Continuity

Covering tuition, transportation, books and after-school tutoring so children remain in school and reach their potential.

Family Accountability

Regular audits ensure the mother and home provide a safe, stable environment. We step in with counselling and referrals to social services if necessary.

Community Integration

Building local partnerships with schools, healthcare providers and volunteer mentors to create a network of predictable support.

Kids We Support

Below are short profiles of some children currently supported by our Scarborough chapter. We publish progress snapshots so donors and volunteers can see measurable results.

Aisha K. — 9 years

Grade: 4   •   Mother: Mrs. Khan

Case: Father passed away due to prolonged illness. Aisha's family lacked funds for tuition and study materials. We provided three terms of tuition and weekly tutoring. Her mathematics grade improved from 45% to 78% in one year.

Needs: Ongoing tutoring and annual school fees.

Rohan S. — 11 years

Grade: 6   •   Mother: Mrs. D'Souza

Case: Rohan's father died in an accident; the family lost part of their livelihood. Rohan struggled with irregular attendance. We provided school travel support and academic mentoring; attendance now exceeds 95% and teachers report improved behaviour and focus.

Needs: Exam fees and summer school tuition.

Mina P. — 8 years

Grade: 3   •   Mother: Mrs. Patel

Case: Mina's father passed unexpectedly. Mina needed early reading support. We placed her in a reading program and assigned a volunteer tutor twice a week. Mina now reads at grade level and participates in class activities confidently.

Needs: Reading materials and holiday camp sponsorship.

How You Can Help

We welcome support at multiple levels — financial, in-kind, and human resources. Below are practical ways to contribute.

Support a Child

Monthly sponsorship helps pay tuition, books and tutoring. Sponsors receive regular progress reports and photos (with guardian consent).

Donate Supplies

School supplies, uniforms and shoes make an immediate difference. We maintain a transparent inventory and publish distribution reports.

Volunteer

Mentor a child, teach after-school classes, or help with administrative tasks. Background checks and orientation are provided for volunteers.

Contact & Referral

If you need to refer a case, volunteer, or donate, please get in touch. For referrals — provide school reports, a short case summary and basic contact details.
